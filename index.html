About Us

Introduction

ST. XAVIER’S COLLEGE, Kathmandu, is an educational institution of higher learning established and managed by the Nepal Jesuit Society. Jesuits began their educational work in Nepal in 1951 with the opening of St. Xavier’s School, Godavari, followed by St. Xavier’s School, Jawalakhel, St. Xavier’s School, Deonia, Moran Memorial School, Maheshpur, and St. Xavier’s School, Sadakbari. The Jesuits have served people of all faiths, all over the world, as educators, scientists, explorers and social reformers since 1540. The centuries-old tradition of service to others is the cornerstone of Jesuit education. Every institute and organization established by the Jesuits in Nepal, prepares each student to Live for God and Lead for Nepal. The education programme is designed to foster criticalthinking, positive action and service to others. It challenges students to look beyond career, and have a wider vision of being channels of betterment. It encourages every student to be a job creator rather than a job seeker, and to play the role of being a creative designer of the future.

OUR MISSION STATEMENT

The Jesuit tradition of educational excellence and service to others guides all the courses and activities at St. Xavier’s College. By integrating academic work and intellectual growth with physical, spiritual and emotional development through various co-curricular activities, the College seeks to foster competence, creativity and innovative spirit, throughout and beyond the years spent in College. At the end of their studies at St. Xavier’s, graduating students are expected to enter the larger world with socially responsible goals, a lifelong appetite for learning and the desire to make our universe a better place. Students are also expected to be prepared to use their God-given talents not only for personal fulfillment, but also for service unto others. The College promotes close student-teacher contact and encourages students to develop their full potential for leadership. The intelligent pursuit of excellence and a variety of activities, which students are exposed to in College, are intended to produce moral leaders who will champion the values of truth, justice and peace.

Therefore, it is our mission to foster...

• Passion for excellence

• Commitment to be compassionate

• Innovative spirit

• Ability to take risks and accept challenges

• Competence

• Creativity

• Leadership for service to all

Research Council- Introduction

COUNCIL

A Council to be known as the "Research Council" is established, which will replace the existing ‘Research Wing’. The ‘Research Wing’ of St. Xavier’s College was instituted on 24 April 2017 with
the guidance and inspiration of Fr. Dr. Vincent Braganza, S.J. (GUJ), the Director of Loyola Center of Research and Development, Ahmedabad, India who provided the college with a road-map in this
frontier work within his one week stay at St. Xavier’s. Dr. Braganza returned on 13 September 2017 to give the fledgling program stronger wings.

OBJECTIVE


The Research Council was established with the aim to advise and assist the Head of College in the leadership and management of research in order to fulfill the research aspects of the College Strategic
Plan. The research Council is directed to provide a conducive environment for promotion and implementation of ‘Research & Innovation’ activities in the College. The research Council is also intended
to support research by means of mentorship, training courses, as well as financial support.

GENERAL DUTIES OF THE COUNCIL


The general duties of the Research Committee are:

    1. Oversee the Academic Governance of research and research training at the College and report to the Academic Council on these matters.
    2. Establish the processes, structures and assign responsibilities to the investigators that collectively:
    a. Achieve effective academic oversight of the quality of research and research training.
    b. Set and monitor institutional benchmarks for research quality and outcomes.
    3. Oversight of academic and research integrity, including monitoring of potential risks
    4. Provide leadership and advice on mechanisms to improve monitoring, benchmarking and reporting of the College’s research performance.
    5. Develop ‘Code of Ethics’ to be followed by the researchers.
    6. Monitoring and reporting to the Academic Council on the quality of research and research training.
    7. Selection of students to involve them in research activities.
    8. Select the innovative research proposals submitted by the faculties or students for funding by the college.
    9. Documentation of the research activities including publication of Journals.
    10. Prepare and review Annual Reports relating to research matters and to make recommendations to the Academic Council.
    11. Develop procedures for allocating research funds and monitor their implementation.
    12. Support the Finance Council in planning and disbursement of research budget.
    13. Conduct research support activities.
    14. Inform and encourage faculty members to apply for Research Projects.
    15. Promotion collaborative research among the Departments.

About library

    Fines:

    An overdue charge of Rs. 1 per book per day will be charged if a book is kept beyond the period of loan.

    1.All Library materials must be properly checked out at the counter before leaving the library. The user is personally responsible for its prompt and safe return and subject to fines and replacement penalties for damaged or lost items.
    2.Any persons found guilty of theft of any Library property will be made accountable as per the College regulations.
    3.Students may come to the Library during any open hours for reading/ reference purposes. Books will be issued only during the respective scheduled time as displayed on the notice board.
    4.Students must stand in Queue to issue books.
    5.The condition of the books must be thoroughly examined by the user at the time of issue. If any damage to the book is found at the time of returning, the book should be replaced by the user.
    6.In case, any book is lost by the user, the user should replace it with the same book if available in the market should pay the cost of the replacement with 10% additional charge of the lost book other book referred by the Library In charge.
    7.Those books which have only limited number of copies will be issued only for shorter periods or may not even be issued. The decision in such cases will be made by the Library staff.
    8.All bags must be kept on the racks at the entrance .During winter season, users should keep their jackets open while leaving the library. If needed users must be ready for inspection at the time of leaving the Library.
    9.Library cannot be held responsible for the loss of user’s personal belongings. Money, mobile, license, ATM cards and other valuables should not be left unattended.
    10. Users should maintain silence inside the library and create a peaceful reading environment for everyone.
    11. A book can be renewed only once by the same user.
    12. Books are issued against the user’s own ID card only. No books will be issued against another person’s card.
    13. Students are encouraged to use the E-library facilities. Computer must be switched off after use.
    14.Ensure that you do not leave your pen drive on the computer.
